# References And Attribution

This repository is independently maintained, but its framing and vocabulary were
informed by adjacent public work on agentic coding, reusable skills, and harness
engineering.

These references are not installable harness assets. They are listed here for
attribution and for readers who want to understand the broader context around
the ideas in this repo.

## Reference Repositories

- [Agentic Coding](https://github.com/agenticoding/agenticoding.github.io)
  by `agenticoding`.
  A course on AI-assisted software development, including methodology,
  workflow design, onboarding, planning, testing, reviewing, and debugging.
  License observed on GitHub: MIT.

- [Skills For Real Engineers](https://github.com/mattpocock/skills)
  by Matt Pocock.
  A public collection of small, adaptable, composable agent skills for real
  engineering work.
  License observed on GitHub: MIT.

- [Learn Harness Engineering](https://github.com/walkinglabs/learn-harness-engineering)
  by `walkinglabs`.
  A project-based course on the environment, state management, verification,
  and control mechanisms that make AI coding agents work reliably.
  License observed on GitHub: MIT.

## Articles And Essays

- [Harness engineering for coding agent users](https://martinfowler.com/articles/harness-engineering.html)
  by Birgitta Boeckeler, published on MartinFowler.com on 2026-04-02.
  A mental model for coding-agent harnesses, including feedforward and feedback
  controls, computational and inferential checks, steering loops, harness
  categories, and the role of human judgment.

- [Effective harnesses for long-running agents](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/effective-harnesses-for-long-running-agents)
  by Justin Young, published by Anthropic on 2025-11-26.
  An engineering write-up on long-running agents, multi-session progress,
  initializer/coding-agent structure, progress artifacts, incremental work, and
  self-verification.

- [Harness design for long-running application development](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/harness-design-long-running-apps)
  by Prithvi Rajasekaran, published by Anthropic on 2026-03-24.
  An engineering write-up on harness design for agentic coding, including task
  decomposition, structured handoffs, context resets, evaluator agents, and
  criteria-driven feedback.
